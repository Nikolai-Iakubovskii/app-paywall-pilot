## Weak signals flagged

1. **Choice Overload effect size**: Iyengar 2000 was n=249 in jam study (small sample). Replication meta-analysis (Scheibehenne 2010, 50+ studies, n=5,036) found near-zero average. Real-world effect exists but is context-dependent. Don't bank on 10x in your A/B.

2. **Fogg B=MAT falsifiability**: Framework explains everything post-hoc; no prediction would falsify it. Useful for design; weak as scientific claim.

3. **Hooked Model causality**: Top apps have hook patterns AND are successful — causal direction unclear. Not academically validated.

4. **Negativity bias mobile-specific magnitude**: "1-star weighs 5x 5-star" is rule-of-thumb derived from general literature, not measured mobile-app finding.

5. **Goal-Gradient onboarding transfer**: Kivetz proved purchase acceleration; whether same dynamic applies to onboarding-quiz completion is plausible but unmeasured.

6. **Identity-based habits empirical support**: Clear's framework intuitive and partially backed by Bem (1972) self-perception, but no rigorous A/B in mobile paywall context.
